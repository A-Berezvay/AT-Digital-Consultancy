What is SEO?

Search engine optimisation (SEO) is the practice of developing a website to improve its ranking in search engine results pages. When increasing search engine ranks, it will increase organic traffic to your website. This means that when somebody searches for a particular word associated with your business, it will position your website further ahead in the search without any paid advertisement. This action drives website traffic, generating leads for your services or products, and potentially introduce you to your future customers.

How Search Engine Works?

The visibility of your page will be highly dependent on the rigorous algorithms built into the search engines. They follow three steps for positioning: screening your pages through:

  1. web crawlers

  2. indexing it to make an understanding of your contents
  3. placing which determines your position on the search page.
  4. We understand how time-consuming and stressful this process is. This is where AT Digital provides hands-on solutions, so you can focus on other aspects of your business while we position you for the right audience.

Crawlers

Search engines use software called web crawlers that find information of your website’s pages using bots (also known as spiders). Google uses Googlebot. This allows them to check millions of web pages instantly. Since website pages and URLs are not registered manually with the search engine, the crawlers are looking for new contents, pages, and updates. This is the first part of the process to get to know your existence in the digital landscape.

Indexing

When your page is crawled by the bots, the search engine starts the second step of its process, which is indexing. This is the stage when the collected data of your pages will be analysed. Indexing will focus on the content of your pages such as texts, headers, images, and activities.

Example:

Imagine this process like sorting files on your computer. When you group certain files into folders, you categorise your data. This is exactly what happens when the search engine indexes your pages. To be successful in this stage, our experts focus passionately on originality, updates, and contents that will help you stand out.

Placing

This is the last step that determines the positioning of your website on the search page. When it comes to placing (ranking) a website's order in the search page, the search engine uses hundreds of different factors to decide the actual position of your page. Although there is a sequence of the steps, step 1 and step 2 are continuously running in the background analysing pages and websites to be able to provide you with real-life data on your search. Therefore, continuous updates on step 1 and step 2 are crucial to be relevant.

Example:

Ranking is best described as you were walking past a bookshop with its latest best-seller book plastered all over the window; upon entering, you saw the very same books on a table in the centre of the hallway, tempting you to come in and take a look before making your decision. Search engines function as digital libraries, continuously analysing website data, traffic, originality, demand, location, and language, summarising it to present its users with the best match for their search.
